~ operations

Flight Operations & Trip Management Software

Airlogical manages every trip end to end — planning and dispatch, handling and permits, in-flight services, and a printable trip pack — with each leg checked for readiness before it flies.

Try NowPricing

A trip is not one decision, it's dozens: which aircraft, which crew, which handler, which permits, what's on board, and whether every one of those is actually in place before the doors close. Most of those decisions are made in email and remembered in someone's head.

Airlogical structures the trip as legs, attaches everything each leg needs, and — critically — evaluates whether the leg is ready to fly. The readiness check draws on the rest of the platform: aircraft status, crew eligibility and recency, flight-time limits, confirmed services and granted permits.

~ capabilities

What the Operations module covers

Trips and legs

Build a trip from its legs, with route, scheduled times, purpose and status through the dispatch workflow.

Crew assignment with eligibility checks

Assign PIC and SIC with type rating, medical, recency and leave validated at the moment of assignment — not discovered later.

Handling, permits and services

Track the handling agent, overflight and landing permits, fuel and catering per leg, with confirmation status visible.

Pre-departure readiness

A per-leg report across aircraft, crew, services, permits and FTL, resolving to ready, needs-attention or not-cleared.

Trip pack

A printable PDF for the crew with the route, times, crew, services, permits and readiness state.

Timeline view

The aircraft's forward legs at a glance for the dispatcher.

~ questions

Questions about Operations

What is trip management in Airlogical?

It's the end-to-end handling of a trip: planning legs, assigning crew, arranging handling and permits, and producing a trip pack — with a readiness check that flags anything that would prevent a leg from flying.

Does it check crew eligibility for a leg?

Yes. Each assignment is checked for type rating, medical validity, landing recency and approved leave, and each leg is checked for aircraft status, services, permits and flight-time limits.

What is the trip pack?

A printable PDF for the crew containing the trip's legs, times, assigned crew, services, permits and the readiness position — everything needed for the flight in one document.

Can it stop a leg that isn't legal?

The readiness check resolves each leg to ready, needs-attention or not-cleared, with blockers such as a missing PIC, an expired type rating or a grounded aircraft called out explicitly.

Does it handle permits and handling agents?

Yes. Overflight and landing permits and handling arrangements are tracked per leg with their confirmation status, and unconfirmed items show in the readiness report.
