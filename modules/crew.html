~ crew

Aircraft Crew Management Software

Airlogical is crew management software for aviation: profiles, licences and ratings, medicals, recency and currency, flight-time limitations and per-aircraft permissions — the complete crew record in one place.

Try NowPricing

Keeping crew legal is a moving target. A licence is valid until it isn't; a medical lapses on a date; recency decays quietly with every week not flown; and duty limits shift with every trip. Tracked in a spreadsheet, these only surface when someone remembers to look.

Airlogical holds the whole crew record — qualifications, medicals, recency and duty — and evaluates it automatically at the point it matters: when a crew member is assigned to a leg, and across the forward schedule so a problem is visible days before it becomes a delay.

~ capabilities

What the Crew module covers

Licences, ratings and type ratings

Held with issue and expiry dates and validated against the aircraft type being flown, so an out-of-type or expired rating is caught before assignment.

Medicals

Class 1, Class 2 and LAPL medicals tracked with expiry, and checked against the operational category — Class 1 where the operation requires it.

Recency and currency

90-day landing recency derived from the legs actually flown, so a pilot who has quietly gone out of currency is flagged rather than assumed current.

Flight-time limitations (FTL)

Duty periods and flight time recorded and evaluated against flight duty period, cumulative and rest requirements, with commander's-discretion recorded where used.

Crew documents

Passports, visas, training records and certificates stored against the profile with expiry tracking.

Per-aircraft permissions

Section-level access per aircraft — a pilot sees their own profile and trips; a manager sees the fleet.

~ questions

Questions about Crew

How does Airlogical track flight-time limitations (FTL)?

Airlogical records crew duty periods and flight time, then evaluates them against the applicable flight duty period, cumulative and rest limits. Crew approaching or exceeding a limit are flagged at the point of assignment and across the fleet schedule.

Does it track licence and medical expiry?

Yes. Licences, ratings, type ratings and medicals are all held with expiry dates, checked against the aircraft and operation, and surfaced in the compliance forecast.

What is landing recency and how is it calculated?

Landing recency is the requirement to have completed a minimum number of take-offs and landings in the preceding 90 days to carry passengers. Airlogical derives it from the legs a pilot actually operated, rather than relying on a manually maintained figure.

Can a pilot only see their own record?

Yes. Permissions are section-level and per aircraft, so a pilot sees their own profile and assigned trips while managers see the fleet.

Does it handle crew across several aircraft?

Yes. Crew can be assigned to multiple tails, and their qualifications are validated against whichever aircraft type a given leg is flown on.
